=== EXPANDED PROJECT: VAKIL AI (DEEP EXPLANATION) ===

Vakil AI is one of the projects I’m the proudest of because it solves a real problem in the Indian legal ecosystem: the inaccessibility of structured legal information. Many people have doubts about laws, rights, and processes, but legal documents are dense, unstructured, and hard to understand. Vakil AI aims to bridge that gap.

I designed the architecture of Vakil AI using a layered AI pipeline:

RAG Layer (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
I built a ChromaDB vector store that stores legal text embeddings with metadata.
When someone asks a legal question, the system retrieves relevant case laws, sections, or statutes and feeds them into the LLM so the answer remains grounded in real documents.

LLM Layer
I experimented with fine-tuned LLaMA models, LoRA adapters, and quantized versions for efficiency.
The idea is to handle long legal text without excessive GPU/CPU overhead.

Reasoning + Structuring Layer
I designed a system where the LLM not only answers questions but also structures responses in a way that resembles real legal reasoning:

stating the relevant section

explaining it clearly

mapping it to the user’s query

giving a structured, citation-backed explanation

Graph Neural Network Layer
I experimented with GNNs and Neo4j to model legal text as a graph.
Legal sections often reference each other, so a graph-based representation helps identify hidden relationships and provide deeper, context-rich answers.

Frontend Layer
Simple, clean UI built for the user to ask queries, get reliable answers, and understand the reasoning clearly.

My aim with Vakil AI is not to replace lawyers, but to give Indian citizens easy, accessible, structured legal insights so they can understand their rights better.
